About FLL Challenge

The FIRST® LEGO® League (FLL) Challenge is a global competition that inspires students to think like scientists and engineers. Teams design, build, and program robots to complete missions on a themed playing field, while also working on an innovative project to solve a real-world problem.

At SunBot Robotics, we prepare our students not just to compete, but to excel in FLL competitions. Our comprehensive training covers all aspects of the competition, from robot design to project research and core values.

Our Competition Journey

From Team Formation to Competition Day

Step 1: Team Formation

Students are organized into teams of 4-6 members. Each team member takes on specific roles including robot designer, programmer, researcher, and project manager. We focus on building strong team dynamics and collaboration skills.

Step 2: Design & Build

Teams work on designing and building their competition robots. This phase includes brainstorming design ideas, testing different configurations, and optimizing the robot for mission completion. Students learn engineering principles through hands-on experience.

Step 3: Practice Missions

Intensive practice sessions where teams program their robots to complete competition missions. Students develop programming skills, learn to debug code, and optimize robot performance. Mock competitions help prepare teams for the real event.

Step 4: Competition Day

The culmination of months of preparation! Teams compete in robot games, present their innovative projects, and demonstrate FLL Core Values. Competition day is about more than winning—it's about learning, teamwork, and celebrating achievements.
